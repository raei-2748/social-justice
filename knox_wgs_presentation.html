CFC Member Analysis

What is driving
the growth?

CFC members were asked to analyse the trend before this presentation. No prompting, no format, just real analysis from Knox students.

Insight 01
The fundraising trajectory has taken a structural upward shift rather than a cyclical pattern. This signals improved participation elasticity and stronger early-stage engagement each year.
Structural shift
Insight 02
A compounding awareness effect is occurring. As cohorts progress through Knox, their exposure accumulates, leading to lower resistance and higher conversion rates over time.
Network effects
Insight 03
Senior students create a network multiplier effect, making participation visible and normalising donation behaviour for younger year groups who look up to them.
Peer effects
Insight 04
To strengthen future campaigns, measure KPIs by year level: participation rate, donor retention rate, and average donation per participant. Total dollar growth can mask a shrinking donor base.
Recommendation

CFC members went beyond the cause and read the annual report. Here is the FY2024-25 breakdown, in plain terms.

Revenue and allocation

Total revenue FY24-25
$39.2M
+30% vs prior year
The trend that matters. In FY23-24, the Foundation ran a $5.8M deficit. In FY24-25, it flipped to a $4.9M surplus. Fundraising costs fell while revenue grew 30%. Each dollar raised now costs less to raise.
CFC verdict: Based on the FY24-25 financials, the Leukaemia Foundation is financially stable and improving. 55% of revenue reaching the mission directly is reasonable for a national charity of this scale. Worth monitoring whether that ratio improves.
Key open question
Is growth driven by more participants or larger average donations? The answer determines how sustainable the trajectory really is.
